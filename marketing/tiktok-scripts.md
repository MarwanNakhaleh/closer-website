## Script 1: "The real reason couples stop being intimate"

**Hook (text on screen):** "It's not that you stopped loving each other."

**Script (45 sec):**
Everyone thinks couples stop being intimate because the spark dies. That's not what happens. What happens is you get busy. You get tired. You stop checking in. And over time, the gap between you gets so wide that bridging it feels awkward. So you don't. And the gap gets wider. The spark didn't die. You just stopped feeding it. The good news? The fix isn't some grand gesture. It's five minutes of honest conversation. Every week. That's it. The couples who stay connected aren't the ones with more chemistry. They're the ones who pay attention.

**Visual direction:** Talking head, casual setting (couch, kitchen). Eye contact with camera. No props needed. Raw, honest delivery. Cut to text cards for key stats.

**Caption:** The spark isn't dead. You're just not feeding it. #couples #relationships #intimacy #connection #closerapp

## Script 2: "We tracked our intimacy for 30 days"

**Hook (text on screen):** "We tracked our intimacy for 30 days. Here's what we learned."

**Script (50 sec):**
So my partner and I decided to track our intimate life for 30 days. Not just frequency. Satisfaction. Who initiated. What we were doing before. How connected we felt. Sounds clinical, right? It wasn't. It was actually wild. Here's what we found. One: our best moments happened after real conversations, not after date nights. Two: I was initiating like 80% of the time, and my partner genuinely didn't realize. Three: we had a natural rhythm of about every 4 to 5 days, and when life pushed it past a week, our whole emotional connection dipped. We couldn't see any of this without the data. Thirty days of tracking taught us more about each other than three years of guessing.

**Visual direction:** Couple together on couch, one holding phone showing a simple chart/graph. Alternate between both partners sharing findings. Natural lighting. Casual clothes.

**Caption:** 30 days of data > 3 years of guessing. #couplesgoals #intimacymatters #relationshiptips #tracking #closerapp

## Script 5: "What happy couples do differently"

**Hook (text on screen):** "Researchers studied 3,000 couples for 40 years. Here's what they found."

**Script (45 sec):**
Happy couples don't fight less. They don't have more sex. They don't have more compatible personalities. What they do have: a 5 to 1 ratio. Five positive interactions for every one negative interaction. That's it. A smile. A touch. Asking a follow-up question. Turning toward each other instead of away. Five small positive moments for every one negative moment. The couples who maintained that ratio stayed together 94% of the time. The ones who didn't? They split. It's not about grand gestures or perfect compatibility. It's about the tiny moments you probably don't even notice. Start noticing.

**Visual direction:** Quick montage of micro-moments: holding hands, making eye contact, small touches, laughing together. Numbers on screen for the 5:1 ratio and 94% stat.

**Caption:** 5:1. That's the ratio. Are you hitting it? #gottman #relationships #science #couples #happycouple #closerapp

## Script 8: "The 5-minute check-in"

**Hook (text on screen):** "5 minutes changed our entire relationship"

**Visual direction:** Text cards showing each question as you say them. Then couple having the check-in (casual, real, maybe slightly awkward at first). End with them laughing together.

**Hook (text on screen):** "There's a difference between busy and disconnected."

**Script (45 sec):**
Busy couples have packed schedules and still make eye contact during dinner. Disconnected couples have all the time in the world and sit in separate rooms. Busy is a schedule problem. ## Production Notes

- All videos should be 30-60 seconds for optimal TikTok performance
- Film vertically (9:16)
- Use trending audio where it fits, but the talking head format works well for relationship content
- Captions/subtitles on all videos (85% of TikTok is watched without sound)
- Post 3-5x per week for growth
- Best posting times for relationship content: 7-9 PM weekdays, 10 AM-12 PM weekends
- Engage with every comment in the first hour after posting
- Batch film: all 15 scripts can be filmed in one afternoon
